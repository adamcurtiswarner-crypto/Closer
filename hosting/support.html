How can we help?

Quick answers to the things people ask most. If yours isn't here, email us — a person reads every message.

My pairing code isn't working

Invite codes are six characters — letters and the digits 2 through 9 — and each code expires after 7 days and links one couple. If the app says the code is invalid or expired, ask your partner to open Stoke and share a fresh invite; a new code takes a few seconds to make.

Still stuck? Check for easy mix-ups first: the letter O is never in a code (nor are 0 and 1), and the code must be entered exactly as sent.

My subscription isn't showing (restore a purchase)

Stoke Premium bills through your Apple ID, and one subscription covers both partners. If you subscribed but the app shows Free, open the upgrade screen and tap Restore purchases, and make sure your device is signed in with the Apple ID that made the purchase.

If your partner is the subscriber, Premium reaches you automatically once you're paired — there's nothing for you to buy.

My partner left Stoke

If your partner deletes their account, we let you know and the pairing ends. Your account and your own answers stay yours. When you're ready, you can pair again with a new invite code — with the same person on a fresh account, or someone new.

Delete my data

In the app, go to Profile → Privacy & Data → Delete account. Your account is deactivated immediately and your data is permanently removed within 30 days. If you want a copy first, use Export my data on the same screen (available once every 24 hours).

You can also anonymize your written responses without deleting your account — same screen.

Notifications aren't arriving

First check iOS Settings → Notifications → Stoke and make sure notifications are allowed. Then in the app, check Profile → Notifications — your daily prompt time and the reminder toggles live there.

Two quiet-by-design things to know: reminders only send between 8am and 9pm your local time, and Stoke never nags — if a day slips, it'll keep.

Anything else

Tell us what's happening and what device you're on — we'll take it from there.
