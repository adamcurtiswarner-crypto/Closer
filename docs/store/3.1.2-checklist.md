# Guideline 3.1.2 Pre-Submit Self-Audit (subscriptions)

*Run this checklist against the actual build and the actual ASC configuration
before every submission. 3.1.2 (auto-renewable subscriptions) is the most
common first-submission rejection for subscription apps; every line below is a
known rejection pattern.*

## In-app paywall (the screen itself)

- [ ] **Trial CTA sits adjacent to the post-trial price.** The button/copy that
  starts the trial ("Try 14 days free") and the price that follows it
  ("then $49.99/year" or "$9.99/month") are visible in the SAME view without
  scrolling, tapping, or expanding. No price hidden behind a toggle.
- [ ] Subscription **length and renewal terms** are stated on the paywall:
  auto-renews, cancel anytime in Apple ID settings, charged at confirmation.
- [ ] **"One subscription covers both partners"** claim matches reality (the
  couple-scoped entitlement in `useSubscription.ts` / the RevenueCat webhook).
- [ ] **Restore Purchases** is present and functional on the paywall (it is —
  `restore()` in `src/hooks/useSubscription.ts`; verify the button is visible
  on the shipped paywall screen, not just implemented).
- [ ] **Terms of Use (EULA) and Privacy Policy links are tappable on the
  paywall** and open successfully in-app.

## Links in-app AND in metadata (both are required)

- [ ] Privacy Policy link reachable in-app (paywall and/or Settings → legal).
- [ ] Terms of Use / EULA link reachable in-app. If using Apple's standard
  EULA, still link it; if custom terms, host and link https://stoke.llc/terms.
- [ ] **App Store Connect metadata**: Privacy Policy URL field filled; Terms of
  Use (EULA) URL in the App Description or the EULA field. (The description's
  SUBSCRIPTION section in STORE_METADATA.md already carries both URLs — keep
  them there.)
- [ ] Both URLs load over public HTTPS **before** submitting (Apple fetches
  them during metadata review). If DNS for stoke.llc is not live, submit with
  the Firebase web.app policy URLs instead — do not wait on DNS.

## ASC subscription configuration

- [ ] **Subscription group correctness:** annual ($49.99/yr, 14-day intro
  trial) and monthly ($9.99/mo) are in the SAME subscription group, so
  upgrading/downgrading works and users cannot double-subscribe.
- [ ] The 14-day free trial is configured as an **Introductory Offer** on the
  annual product in ASC (not just RevenueCat copy) and matches every claim in
  the listing ("14-day free trial, then $49.99 per year").
- [ ] Both products have **review screenshots** attached (clears the
  "Missing Metadata" state).
- [ ] Both subscriptions are **attached to the version** on the App Store
  version page (first submission requires this explicitly).
- [ ] Product display names/prices in ASC match the paywall copy exactly.

## Submission mechanics

- [ ] **Manual release selected** ("Manually release this version") — per the
  launch plan, the 10–20 couple beta runs during Apple's review wait and we
  release on exit criteria, not on approval.
- [ ] **Paid Apps Agreement = Active** in Agreements, Tax, and Banking (W-9
  complete, banking verified). This is a hard gate: subscriptions cannot go
  live — and review can stall — without it. Longest pole; check it first.

## Sandbox verification (do this on-device before submitting)

- [ ] Sign in with a **sandbox Apple ID** on a device build; open the paywall.
- [ ] Start the annual trial: confirm the StoreKit sheet shows "14 days free,
  then $49.99/year".
- [ ] Confirm the purchase completes and premium unlocks in-app immediately
  (RevenueCat entitlement `premium`).
- [ ] Confirm the RevenueCat **webhook fires** and writes
  `/subscriptions/{uid}` (status `active`, `couple_id` set) and the couple's
  `premium_until` mirror — then confirm the **partner account** resolves
  premium without purchasing (the "both of you" claim).
- [ ] Test **Restore Purchases** on a reinstall/second device with the same
  sandbox ID.
- [ ] Test the monthly product purchase path once.
- [ ] Verify the demo reviewer couple is staged (`seedReviewerCouple.ts`) so
  the reviewer never needs to purchase to see the app.
