## MARKET OVERVIEW

**Market Size:** ~$2B (2024-2025), projected to reach $5.77B by 2033 at ~12.5% CAGR.

**Growth Drivers:**
- Increasing normalization of seeking digital support for relationships
- AI-powered coaching and personalized recommendations
- Subscription model maturation in health/wellness apps
- Post-dating-app lifecycle: couples need tools *after* matching
- Rising teletherapy adoption extending into preventive relationship wellness

**Key Macro Trends (2025-2026):**
- AI companion apps surged 700% between 2022 and mid-2025
- Dating app installs dropped 4% and sessions fell 7% in 2025 — but *relationship maintenance* apps are growing
- Both app stores now weight retention over raw downloads in ranking algorithms
- Google shifted its algorithm from install volume to retention (2025)
- Apple began indexing screenshot caption text for keyword ranking (June 2025)
- Digital mental health platforms projected to grow from $0.89B (2025) to $2.49B (2034)

## COMPETITOR PROFILES

### 1. PAIRED
**Company:** Paired (London, UK). $3.6M seed round (2021).
**Tagline:** "Build a better relationship in 5 minutes a day"

**Core Features:**
- Daily questions designed by relationship therapists — both partners answer, then unlock each other's responses
- 128+ question packs (e.g., The Orgasm Pack, Family Traditions, Married Couples Check-Up)
- Structured programs on communication, intimacy, conflict management
- Quizzes, games, relationship timeline
- Some video-format questions

**Pricing:** Free tier (limited). Premium: $14.99/month or $69.99-$74.99/year. If one partner gets Premium, the other gets access.

**App Store Performance:**
- Rating: 4.59/5 (Android, ~60K ratings)
- Downloads: ~8 million total (official); ~4.7M on Android per AppBrain
- Est. monthly revenue: ~$200K (App Store alone, per Sensor Tower)
- Google Play Award winner 2020 (Personal Growth), Apple App of the Day (Jan 2024)

**Unique Angle:** Therapist-designed content with the polish and habit-forming mechanics of a consumer app. Positioned as the category leader.

**Strengths:**
- Highest brand recognition in category
- Strong content depth (128+ themed question packs)
- Award-winning, well-designed UX
- Therapist credibility
- Both partners share one subscription

**Weaknesses:**
- Expensive — users frequently complain about pricing
- Aggressive paywall and misleading "free" marketing
- Limited free tier creates churn before value is demonstrated
- No AI coaching component
- Content can feel repetitive after months

**What Paired has that Stoke doesn't:** Structured multi-session programs/courses, video-format questions, 128+ themed question packs, quiz/game mechanics.

**What Stoke has that Paired doesn't:** [Stoke's unique differentiators — daily prompts with "Tend your relationship" positioning, specific features TBD based on Stoke's roadmap]

### 2. LASTING

**What Stoke has that Lasting doesn't:** Lighter daily touch, preventive/wellness framing (vs. repair), likely more approachable for couples not in crisis.

### 4. AGAPE
**Company:** Agape (getdailyagape.com)

**App Store Performance:**

**Strengths:**

**What Agape has that Stoke doesn't:** Multi-relationship-type support (not just romantic), question personalization by relationship context, polyamory support.

## RETENTION & CHURN INSIGHTS

### Industry Benchmarks
- Dating/relationship app Day 30 retention: ~3.3% (2024)
- 20-25% of dating app users drop off *weekly*
- Majority of churn occurs within the first month
- Less than 5% of monthly subscribers still active at 12 months

### Why Couples App Users Churn
1. **Broken onboarding** — Users who don't experience core value in Session 1 rarely return
2. **Lack of perceived value** — If prompts feel generic or repetitive, engagement drops fast
3. **Boredom / content exhaustion** — Most apps have 3-6 months of unique content before recycling
4. **One partner disengages** — If one person stops, the other follows (unique two-sided churn risk)
5. **"We're fixed" effect** — Couples in good shape feel they no longer need the app
6. **Price sensitivity after trial** — Aggressive paywalls after free trials cause immediate churn

### What Drives Highest Retention
1. **Daily habit loop** — Apps that create a consistent small daily moment outperform session-based ones
2. **Both-partner engagement** — The reveal mechanic (answer then see partner's answer) is the strongest engagement driver
3. **Personalized content** — Generic prompts churn faster than contextual ones
4. **Streaks** — Paired and others use daily streaks; they work but can feel shallow if not tied to real value
5. **Progress visualization** — Love Nudge's Love Tank, Flamme's habit tracker — seeing growth keeps users engaged
6. **Varied content types** — Mix of questions, activities, quizzes, and challenges prevents fatigue
7. **Onboarding quality** — Users who experience core benefit in first session have dramatically higher retention

### Clinical vs. Gamified
A couples therapist review notes an important distinction: "Most relationship app categories — gamified couple trivia, streak-based daily check-ins with no depth — don't move the needle clinically. They feel productive without changing anything." The apps that work best **combine** clinical grounding with engagement mechanics. Pure gamification without depth churns. Pure clinical without engagement churns. The sweet spot is both.

## AI COACHING TREND ANALYSIS

### Current State (2026)
- AI coaching is the fastest-growing feature in the couples app space
- Flamme, Connected, and CoupleWork are leading with AI integration
- A March 2025 NEJM AI study validated therapeutic AI chatbots (Therabot) for anxiety/depression
- BMC Psychology 2025 meta-analysis confirmed digital interventions improve relationship satisfaction

### What AI Can Do Well
- Personalized daily prompts based on couple history
- Conflict de-escalation suggestions
- Communication pattern analysis
- Date planning and activity suggestions
- Psychoeducation delivery

### What AI Cannot Do (Yet)
- Hold space for two people simultaneously in real-time
- Navigate live emotional ruptures
- Handle high-conflict or abusive dynamics safely
- Replace the therapeutic alliance
- Work with more than one person's perspective at a time

### Strategic Implication for Stoke
AI coaching is becoming table stakes. Not having it will increasingly feel like a gap. But the implementation matters: AI should enhance the daily prompt experience (better personalization, contextual follow-ups) rather than try to be a therapist. The winning framing is "AI-enhanced daily connection" not "AI therapy."

## STRATEGIC TAKEAWAYS FOR STOKE

### 1. Market Positioning
Stoke occupies a specific and defensible niche: **daily prompts for established couples (2+ years, 25-45) who want to stay connected preventively** — not repair. This differentiates from:
- Paired (broader, more clinical)
- Lasting (crisis/repair oriented)
- Flamme (younger, gamified, AI-heavy)
- Agape (minimalist, multi-relationship)
- Between (utility, not growth)
- Ours (full therapy)

The "Tend your relationship" tagline captures the gardening metaphor perfectly. This is about *maintenance and growth*, not fixing something broken.

### 2. Competitive Gaps Stoke Can Exploit
- **Paired is expensive and paywalled** — Stoke can win on value and free-tier generosity
- **Lasting is clinical and heavy** — Stoke can win on lightness and daily accessibility
- **Agape is too minimal** — Stoke can offer more without overwhelming
- **Relish is dying** — Its coaching-oriented users need a new home
- **Love Nudge is dated** — Stoke can modernize the daily-habit approach
- **No one owns the "2+ years, not in crisis" positioning** — This is Stoke's to claim

### 3. Features That Matter Most for Retention
Based on competitive analysis, prioritize:
1. **Answer-and-reveal mechanic** (proven by Paired and Agape)
2. **Content depth and variety** (prevent content exhaustion — the #1 long-term churn driver)
3. **Streak/progress visualization** (but tie to real meaning, not hollow gamification)
4. **Personalization** (questions contextual to relationship length, stage, history)
5. **AI-enhanced prompts** (becoming table stakes — plan for this)
6. **Generous free tier** (Paired's aggressive paywall is its biggest weakness — be the opposite)

### 4. Threats to Watch
- **Flamme** is the most direct emerging threat: AI coaching + gamification + $39.99/year is compelling
- **Connected** offering 30+ tools for free is aggressive — watch for their monetization pivot
- **Big platform entry** — BetterHelp, Talkspace, or Calm could build a couples daily-prompt feature tomorrow
- **AI-native startups** — CoupleWork and others building AI-first may leapfrog traditional prompt apps

### 5. Pricing Strategy Insights
| Competitor | Monthly | Annual | Model |
|-----------|---------|--------|-------|
| Paired | $14.99 | $69.99-74.99 | Freemium, aggressive paywall |
| Lasting | $29.99-39.99 | N/A | Freemium, premium heavy |
| Flamme | $11.99 | $39.99 | Freemium, generous free |
| Agape | ~$4.17/mo | $39.99-50 | Freemium |
| Between | $2.99 | $13.99 | Free + cosmetic upgrades |

**Sweet spot for Stoke:** $7.99-9.99/month or $49.99-59.99/year. Undercut Paired meaningfully while being premium enough to signal quality. Generous free tier with enough value that couples experience the core benefit before being asked to pay.
